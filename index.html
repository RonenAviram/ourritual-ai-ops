The engine behind the dashboard

The AI Overlay is a middleware layer that sits between raw data sources (CRM, platform analytics, session recordings) and the operations team. Instead of reacting to problems after they surface, it identifies risk patterns early and recommends action before a therapist churns.

Architecture Overview

A Claude Desktop methodology folder — every component is purpose-built for the retention workflow

.claude/
├── CLAUDE.md — Methodology brain: defines the 5-phase cycle, data simulation rules, and behavioral constraints
├── skills/ — 7 specialized modules, each a self-contained capability:
│   ├── intelligence-gathering — Cross-references CRM, platform, and session data per therapist
│   ├── risk-assessment — Scores churn probability across 5 weighted dimensions
│   ├── intervention-plan — Generates a personalized talk-track for the retention conversation
│   ├── conversation-debrief — Processes call outcomes and updates the risk model
│   ├── followup-loop — Tracks commitments, schedules check-ins, measures recovery
│   ├── data-simulation — Generates realistic therapist data when live systems aren't connected
│   └── phase-enforcement — Ensures human approval between phases (no autonomous escalation)
├── agents/ — 2 sub-agents for compute-heavy tasks:
│   ├── risk-scoring-agent — Dedicated risk computation engine with weighted formula
│   └── debrief-analysis-agent — Post-conversation sentiment and outcome analysis
├── commands/ — Quick-start entry points:
│   ├── /retention-start — Begin a new case from a name and a feeling
│   ├── /retention-status — Check progress on active cases
│   └── /retention-end — Close a case with outcome documentation
├── references/ — Domain knowledge:
│   └── churn-indicators — 5 archetypes (Frustrated Operator, Neglected Partner, Outgrowing Practitioner, Burned Out, Price Sensitive)
└── templates/ — Structured output formats for each phase deliverable
